# Data Wishlist for DFAST Analysis

This document lists additional data that would be helpful for a more in-depth analysis of the DFAST results.

*   **Historical financial statements for each bank:** This would allow for the calculation of a wider range of financial ratios (e.g., ROA, ROE, LCR, NSFR) and a more detailed time series analysis. The ideal format would be a CSV file with quarterly data going back to at least 2013.

*   **Detailed DFAST model documentation:** While the publicly available information provides a good overview of the model evolution, having the detailed model documentation would allow for a much deeper understanding of the models and their assumptions. This would likely be in the form of technical papers or manuals from the Federal Reserve.

*   **More granular data on loan portfolios:** Having more detailed data on the loan portfolios of each bank would allow for a more granular analysis of credit risk. This could include:
    *   Geographic distribution of loans
    *   Credit scores of borrowers
    *   Loan-to-value ratios for mortgages
    *   Industry concentration for commercial loans

*   **Data on the evolution of bank business models over time:** The current data provides a snapshot of the business models at a few points in time. Having more historical data on revenue, profit, and assets by business segment would allow for a more detailed analysis of how the business models have evolved and how this has impacted their DFAST results.

*   **Liquidity data:** Data to calculate liquidity ratios such as the Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) and the Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR). This would require detailed data on the banks' high-quality liquid assets (HQLA), net cash outflows, available stable funding, and required stable funding.
