## Category 1: Classic Banking Histories (Items 1-14)

**Why these matter**: The dissertation's institutional analysis requires grounding in the long arc of American banking regulation. These pre-1950 and mid-century histories provide the baseline against which modern regulatory architecture is measured.

**Acquisition strategy**:
- **Public domain works** (pre-1924): Knox (1900), Bolles (1886-92), Sumner (1896), Catterall (1903), Holdsworth (1910), White (1911) — all freely available via Internet Archive, HathiTrust, or Google Books
- **FRASER archive**: The St. Louis Fed's FRASER system holds many National Monetary Commission reports including Holdsworth
- **Out-of-print mid-century works**: Redlich (1947-51), Smith (1953), Studenski (1952), Helderman (1931) — university interlibrary loan or used book dealers (AbeBooks, Alibris)
- **In-print reprints**: Rothbard (2002) free from Mises Institute; Timberlake (1993) from UChicago Press

**Cost estimate**: ~$0 for public domain; ~$50-150 for used copies of mid-century works
